Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee

The man who made the world more open and accessible of information by inventing World Wide Web.

Some Important Events of Sir Tim Berners-Lee's Life

  • Sir Tim was born on 8th June, 1955 and his birth place was London,United Kingdom.
  • His other names TimBL or TBL.
  • His parents are Conway Berners-Lee and Mary Lee Woods.
  • He got Bachelor of Arts in Physics at The Queen's college,Oxford in 1976.
  • In 1978,he joined D.G.Nash in Ferndown,Dorset and helped in creating type-setting software for printers.
  • He became an Independent contractor at CERN,the largest internet node in Europe.
  • In 1989,the time came,when Sir Tim Berners-Lee invented WWW at CERN.
  • Sir Tim put the first website online for the first time on 6th August,1991.
  • He founded the World Wide Web Consortium at Massachusetts Institute of Technology and become the dirctor of W3C,in 1994.
  • In 2001,Sir Berners-Lee was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society.
  • He was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II in the 2004 New Year Honours for services to the global development of the internet and was invested formally on 16 July 2014.
  • In the year 2007,he was appointed the Order of Merit.
  • He was announced to work with UK Government by the British Prime Minister,Sir James Gordon Brown,in 2009.In the same year Sir Tim launced the World Wide Web Foundation.
  • He was honoured with the Queen Elizabeth Prize in 2013.
  • He also received the famous Turing Award from The Association for Computing Machinery for his invention(WWW) in 2016.
  • Currently Sir Tim Berners-Lee is working as a computer science professor at University of Oxford and MIT.
